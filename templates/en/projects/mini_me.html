How it started

I thought it would be cool to have a robot small humanoid robot that could mimic my movements. I investigated about existing alternatives, and I found that all humanoid robots available on the market were expensive, bipedal (which made them very unstable), or/and closed-source and closed-hardware, which meant that they were quickly falling behind new technologies and advancements in computing hardware. It was then when I started thinking of making a small humanoid robot myself, with wheels or threads instead of legs, fully open-source, and as cheap and easy to build as possible.

The goals

  • Make a mini humanoid robot, powered by a standard Arduino micro-controller.
  • Make it as cheap as possible.
  • Make an exoskeleton to make it mimic movements.
  • Make it easy to build, expand and modify, to allow people to go way beyond the base provided by the Arduino and experiment with autonomous control.
  • If I manage to achieve all of that, the ultimate goal would be to foster an open-source community for the continued development of the robot, and to transform it into an accesible and inexpensive platform for education and research.

Project status

The project is now paused. I managed to get a working prototype, but I wasn't able to go beyond it for a lack of time, money and knowledge about electronics. I have since learned a lot of things and hope to continue this project in the future.
